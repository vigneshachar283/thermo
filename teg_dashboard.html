Abstract

This project presents the design and simulation of a thermoelectric waste heat energy harvesting system using thermoelectric generators (TEG). A significant amount of thermal energy generated in automotive engines and industrial systems is wasted in the form of heat. The proposed system aims to recover a portion of this waste heat and convert it into useful electrical energy using the Seebeck effect.

Components Used

  • TEC1-12706 Module: A Peltier module used in reverse (Seebeck mode) to generate electricity from temperature gradients.
  • SX1308 Boost Converter: A high-frequency boost converter used to step up the low TEG output voltage to a regulated level (e.g., 5V) for powering electronics.
  • Heat Sinks: Crucial for maintaining the temperature difference (ΔT) by cooling the "cold side."

Mathematical Principles

Seebeck Effect: V = α × ΔT

Where α is the Seebeck coefficient and ΔT is the temperature difference.

The system's efficiency depends on the thermal conductivity of the TEG material and the efficiency of the power conditioning stage (SX1308). In this implementation (Modules in Series), we use a composite Seebeck coefficient of 0.052V/°C, achieving a peak power of 4.16W at ΔT=100°C as shown in our experimental results.
